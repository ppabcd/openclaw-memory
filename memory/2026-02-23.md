## Research: ARF Epidemiology

User requested research on Acute Rheumatic Fever epidemiology (Indonesia & worldwide):

**Indonesia:**
- 4th highest prevalence globally (1.18M cases/year)
- Mortality: 4.8 per 100k (higher than malaria)
- Prevalence in school-age children: 0.3-0.8%
- 8-year survival: only 60%
- Main predictors of death: poor penicillin adherence (HR 12.7), CHF (HR 18.6)
- **Problem**: No national RHD program in Indonesia (neglected disease)

**Global:**
- ~470,000 new cases/year
- Incidence: 8-51 per 100k
- Highest burden: developing countries (South Asia, Sub-Saharan Africa)
- Age: 5-15 years most affected

**Sources provided:**
- Arafuri et al. (2022) - PMC9562774 (Indonesian children survival study)
- Watkins et al. (2017) - N Engl J Med (Global burden data)
- NCBI StatPearls (2025) - NBK594238
- PubMed (2021) - PMID 33931507
- BMJ Heart (2008) - systematic review
- Alomedika - Indonesian epidemiology
